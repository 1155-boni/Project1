Me in Person

Vybz Kartel, the Jamaican dancehall star, was arrested in 2011 and convicted of murder in 2014, but his conviction was overturned by a UK court in March 2024, leading to his release from prison in July 2024. Here's a more detailed account of Vybz Kartel's story: Arrest and Trial: Vybz Kartel, whose real name is Adidja Palmer, was arrested in 2011 and charged with the murder of his associate, Clive "Lizard" Williams. Conviction and Sentence: After a 65-day trial, one of the longest in Jamaican history, he was convicted in 2014 and sentenced to life in prison with a minimum of 35 years, which was later reduced to 32 and a half years on appeal. Overturned Conviction: In March 2024, the UK Judicial Committee of the Privy Council quashed Kartel's conviction, citing juror misconduct and stating that the Jamaican Court of Appeal had to decide if a retrial would take place. Release from Prison: On July 31, 2024, after the Jamaican Court of Appeal declined to retry the case, Vybz Kartel was released from prison, having spent over 10 years behind bars. Health Issues: Kartel's health was a factor in the decision not to retry the case, with reports suggesting he was in declining health. Ongoing Legal Matters: While Kartel was released from prison, he is still facing legal issues related to the case, including potential charges related to attempting to pervert the course of justice.
